         Research Focus
      

      
Dr BSVP specializes in the development of tools for fluid-structure-interaction (FSI) in Fluid-Thermal systems. His group has worked on wide spectrum of fluid-structure interaction problems ranging from the dynamics of wakes past bluff bodies to flow through the circulatory systems. Algorithms have been developed to control the self-excited nature of flow past bodies and resulting flow induced vibrations (FIV). The focus has been on problems such as, blast loading of structures (using smooth particle hydrodynamics - SPH) to micro-flow simulations using dissipative particle dynamics ‚Äì DPD. Of late, his group is interested in analyzing problems of clinical interest such as, arterial wall coupling with the dynamics of blood flow in the cardio-vascular flows and cerebral circulation. Current interest also includes calculation of CHF margins in rod bundle heat transfer and their vibrations.
